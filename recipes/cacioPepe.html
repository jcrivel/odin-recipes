Cacio e Pepe

Description

This cacio e pepe recipe is easy to make in about 30 minutes with just 4 simple ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces bucatini
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon frech ground black pepper
  • 2 ounces finely-grated Pecorino-Romano cheese

Steps

  1. Fill a large stockpot about halfway full of water (roughly 3 quarts) and bring it to a rolling boil. Generously season the water with fine sea salt (about 2 tablespoons).
  2. Meanwhile, as the pasta water heats, melt the butter in a large sauté pan (preferably nonstick) over medium heat. Add the pepper and let it cook for 30 seconds, then turn off the heat. If you have an electric stove, you’ll want to remove the pan from the hot burner entirely.
  3. Add the pasta to the boiling water and cook, stirring occasionally, until it is just barely al dente.
  4. Use tongs* to quickly transfer the pasta directly to the sauté pan with the melted butter and pepper. Add 1/3 cup of the starchy pasta water to the pan and toss the pasta briefly to combine. Add in half of the cheese and toss the pasta briefly to coat. Add in the remaining half of the cheese and continue tossing the pasta, adding in a few extra tablespoons of starchy pasta water if needed to thin out the sauce, until the cheese is melted and forms a smooth and glossy sauce.
  5. Serve the pasta immediately, garnished with an extra twist of black pepper and/or extra Pecorino if desired. This pasta is definitely best served hot out of the pan, so please enjoy it right away!
